What pulled me into embedded work is the stakes involved. I spent a year writing motor protection firmware for industrial pump stations, where a missed deadline or an unhandled fault means real hardware damage, not a graceful failure. That's where I learned what "correct under interrupt" actually means, and it's the kind of pressure I want to keep working under.

I'm fast, I test what I ship, and I'm comfortable being handed a datasheet and a sensor and figuring out the rest.

I'm also teaching myself embedded Linux development, currently working through Mastering Embedded Linux Programming by Frank Vasquez and Chris Simmonds to build a deeper understanding of Linux systems.
